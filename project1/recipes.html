Garlic Butter Chicken with Creamy Spinach (Ketogenic)

Ingredients:
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)

Cooking Instructions:

Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper, then sear them for about 4-5 minutes on each side until golden brown and fully cooked. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside. In the same skillet, reduce the heat to medium and add butter. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for about a minute until fragrant. Pour in the heavy cream and Parmesan cheese, stirring continuously until the sauce begins to thicken. Add the Italian seasoning and red pepper flakes if using, then stir in the fresh spinach and cook until wilted. Return the chicken to the skillet, coating it with the creamy garlic butter sauce, and let it simmer for another 2-3 minutes. Serve hot and enjoy!


Chickpea & Spinach Curry (Vegan)

Ingredients:
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup fresh spinach
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Cooking Instructions:

Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and saut√© for 3-4 minutes until soft and translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for another minute until fragrant. Add the cumin, turmeric, garam masala, paprika, and chili powder, stirring constantly to toast the spices. Pour in the diced tomatoes and cook for 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend. Add the chickpeas, vegetable broth, and coconut milk, then let the curry simmer for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Once the sauce has thickened slightly, stir in the fresh spinach and cook until wilted. Season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice for brightness. Serve hot with rice or naan and enjoy!
