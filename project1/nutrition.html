Nutrition Guide


Mediterranean Diet

The Mediterranean diet focuses on whole foods like fruits, vegetables, whole grains, nuts, fish, and olive oil, while limiting processed foods and red meat. It’s great for heart health, reducing inflammation, and lowering the risk of chronic diseases like diabetes and high blood pressure. This diet is also rich in healthy fats and antioxidants, making it a great option for overall well-being.


Ketogenic

The keto diet is a high-fat, low-carb diet that encourages the body to enter a state of ketosis, where it burns fat for energy instead of carbohydrates. This diet is beneficial for weight loss, blood sugar control, and improving insulin sensitivity. It may also provide mental clarity and steady energy levels, though it requires strict carb restriction.


Vegan Diet

A vegan diet eliminates all animal products, including meat, dairy, and eggs, focusing on plant-based foods. It’s excellent for heart health, reducing cholesterol levels, and promoting environmental sustainability. A well-balanced vegan diet can provide plenty of nutrients while reducing the risk of certain diseases, but it requires careful planning to ensure enough protein, B12, and iron intake.


Paleo Diet

The paleo diet emphasizes eating foods that early humans consumed, such as lean meats, fish, fruits, vegetables, nuts, and seeds, while avoiding processed foods, dairy, and grains. It’s great for reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and stabilizing blood sugar levels. This diet encourages whole, unprocessed foods, which can lead to better energy levels and weight management.
