Workout Guide


Strength Training

Strength training helps build and maintain muscle, which is essential for overall strength, bone health, and metabolism. Lifting weights or using resistance exercises improves posture, enhances joint stability, and reduces the risk of injury. It also increases muscle mass, which can help with fat loss by boosting the bodyâ€™s calorie-burning ability even at rest.


Cardio

Cardio workouts improve heart health, lung capacity, and overall endurance. Activities like running, cycling, and swimming help lower blood pressure, improve circulation, and reduce the risk of heart disease. Additionally, cardio is a great way to burn calories, manage weight, and boost mood through the release of endorphins.


HIIT (High Interval Training)

HIIT is a time-efficient workout that combines short bursts of intense exercise with rest periods, maximizing calorie burn and metabolic rate. It improves cardiovascular fitness while also building muscle endurance. HIIT is particularly effective for fat loss, as it keeps the body burning calories even after the workout ends (known as the afterburn effect).


Flexibility & Mobility Training

Flexibility and mobility training improve range of motion, balance, and muscle recovery. Practices like yoga and Pilates help reduce muscle stiffness, prevent injuries, and enhance posture. These workouts also promote relaxation and stress relief, making them beneficial for both physical and mental well-being.
